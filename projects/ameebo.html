Context

Ameebo is a plush, open-ended toy for children aged 1-6. It combines the proven cognitive benefits of open-ended play, with the comfort and security of a companion toy to create an engaging and fun experience.

This studio project focussed on design for play, and pushed us to utilize user research tactics to find and solve real issues in the market place by creating novel and beautiful objects.

Prompt

Design an open-ended toy.

Playing is learning. it is an essential tool used by humans to understand core concepts of creativity, problem solving, socialization, and more.

The play industry has been around for centuries. In recent years, however, many issues have become apparent in regards to the impact these objects make on the developmental growth of children. The market has become saturated with overly branded and unsustainable products that lack the focus and care necessary when working with these types of users.

Research

Defining Play

Play is the work of children.

Activities deemed as play have a slew behavioral, emotional, social, and cognitive rewards. The benefits received from each vary based on the activity in question, and can either provide additional support in certain categories, or plateau prior development.

These categorizations can help indicate the components of certain activities that may have a more unique cognitive interaction. What is open-ended play?

Open-ended play is play without restrictions.

Objects and activities that fall into this category of play can be used in countless ways without dictating to the user a specific process by which it should be used. This type of play is highly stimulating and creative, and has been shown to provide benefits beyond what children can already expect through traditional play. Specifically, this type of play helps foster imagination, spark creativity, promote problem solving skills, improve cognitive skills, and much more.

Targeted Research

To build a stronger understanding of the industry and how users interact with it, I utilized large scaled user surveys, interviews with purchasers and users, and general industry research. Through these, I was able to identify some key themes that would support my ideation.

Playing is Learning

Children learn everything through play. Their cognitive skills, physical abilities, vocabulary, social skills, and literacy are all facilitated and enhanced through play.

Play is commercialized

Play, and toys, are a commercial industry that can at times prioritize profits over seeking to enhance the development and quality of life of their users.

Categories of play matter

Toys are categorized by the type of play they foster, and how those categories aid childhood development. Studies have shown that the most beneficial and unique toys are those that blur the lines between categories, or envelop multiple.

Abstraction is key

Through market research, interviews, and user testing I was able to discern that abstraction is a key way to push kids towards open-ended play, and needs to be a key focus for both the form and function of the product.

Testing

Comfort without context

Given the product market I was studying was already so developed, I had a lot of freedom to target my testing and research to more niche areas of play that would influence my process. Here I chose to focus on the concept of abstraction, and sought to recognize how children in this age demographic understand, handle, and utilize a lack of context in play.

The test I designed sought to verify that children can create their own context within ambiguity. This test provided kids with a sheet of paper with a simple random pen stroke through the middle of the page, hundreds of these pages were prepared before hand. The kids were then asked to help me ‚Äòfinish‚Äô all my drawings. The goal here was to see what they might see in a single stroke, and what kind of world they might build around this ambiguity.

Prototyping

Ameebo‚Äôs prototyping process was two-fold. I not only had to prototype the form and interactions that the final piece would embody, but simultaneously, I had to learn to sew.

Production

Ameebo's pattern was designed iterativley with paper mockups, these iterations were intended to help define scale of the final piece.

The final prototype was constructed using a combination of cotton and polyester. Though these fabrics and colors were chosen for the prototype, Ameebo can be imagined in countless variations of fabrics and finishes.
